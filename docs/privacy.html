Things Mailman Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

Things Mailman is an open-source macOS utility developed by Maxime de Chalendar. It forwards messages you select in Apple Mail to the private Things address you provide. Things Mailman has no developer-operated server and does not include advertising, analytics, tracking, or third-party SDKs.

Information handled by the app

Things Mailman handles the following information solely to provide its features:

  • Your Things Mailman address. The private @things.email address you enter is stored locally in the app's sandboxed preferences.
  • App preferences. Your keyboard shortcut, launch-at-login choice, menu bar visibility, setup status, and per-account filing preferences are stored locally on your Mac.
  • Selected Mail messages. When you invoke the app, it asks Apple Mail to forward the messages you selected. A forwarded message may include its subject, sender and recipient information, body, headers, and attachments. Apple Mail sends the forward using your configured mail account.
  • Session history. Message subjects, Mail account identifiers, and processing results may appear in the app's history for the current session. This history is held in memory and is cleared when the app quits.
  • Local diagnostics. The app writes operational status, counts, and error codes to Apple's unified logging system. These logs do not include message contents or your Things Mailman address and are not sent to the developer.

Data collection and tracking

The developer does not receive or collect your Mail messages, Things Mailman address, preferences, usage history, or diagnostic logs. Things Mailman does not sell personal information, create advertising profiles, or track you across apps or websites.

This policy page is hosted by GitHub Pages. GitHub may automatically process technical information about visits under its privacy statement. The developer has not added analytics or advertising to this page and does not receive visitor logs from Things Mailman.

Forwarding to Things

Sending a selected message is an action you initiate. Things Mailman directs Apple Mail to forward that message to the private address you supplied. The message is transported by your email provider and received and processed by Cultured Code's Mail to Things service. Cultured Code states that Mail to Things collects the content of forwarded emails and protects personal information as described in its privacy policy.

Things Mailman is an independent project and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Cultured Code. The developer does not control the retention or deletion of information held by your email provider or Cultured Code.

Mail automation permission

Things Mailman requests macOS permission to control Apple Mail. It uses this permission to inspect your current selection, create and send forwards, discover filing locations, and perform the filing action you configured after Mail accepts a forward. You can revoke this permission at any time in System Settings under Privacy & Security > Automation.

Retention and deletion

  • Session history is deleted when Things Mailman quits.
  • Preferences remain on your Mac until you change them or remove the app's data.
  • To remove all locally stored Things Mailman data, quit the app and delete ~/Library/Containers/com.maximedechalendar.ThingsMailman.
  • To manage or delete information stored by Things Cloud, use the controls provided by Things or contact Cultured Code as described in its privacy policy.

Because the developer does not operate a server or receive your data, there is no remote Things Mailman account or developer-held personal data to delete.
